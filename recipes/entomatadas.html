Entomatadas

A classic Mexican dish, consisting in tortillas dipped/smothered in a tomato and chile sauce. Recipe calls for chicken, but it is optional. Serves two.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg of tortilla
  • 1/2 of a chicken breast
  • 1/2 an onion
  • 4 tomatoes
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 sprig of epazote
  • 1 avocado
  • Fresh chiles (al gusto)
  • 1/4 kg of queso panela
  • A nub of butter
  • A pinch of chicken boullion powder

Steps

  1. Bring chiles and tomatoes to a boil, leave boiling until thoroughly cooked. (~10 minutes)
  2. Add chicken to separate pot of boiling water, with a smashed garlic clove and some onion for taste.
  3. Once the chiles and tomatoes are cooked, empty them into a blender with a little bit of water, a few slices of onion, and a dash of chicken boullion powder. Blend thoroughly.
  4. In a deep skillet heat enough oil to thinly cover the bottom. Once shimmering, empty the contents of the blender. Cook on medium heat until tomato flavor develops (~6 minutes.
  5. In the meantime,
    • Fry desired number of tortillas in butter until cooked but not crispy.
    • Shred chicken breast.
  6. Throw the sprig of epazote into the sauce and stir. Remove sooner for less epazote flavor and later for more.
  7. Roll chicken into tortillas, serve on plate, and cover in sauce.
  8. Top with avocado and cheese. (Try raw onion, cream, or parsley as well
